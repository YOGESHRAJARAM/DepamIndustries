This page provides details on different types of wall gates used for property access control.

Types of Wall Gates

  • Swing Gate

    The most common type. Hinges on one side and swings open. Can have single or double leaves.

  • Sliding Gate

    Slides parallel to the wall on a track. Ideal for narrow driveways or limited swinging space.

  • Folding Gate (Bi-Fold Gate)

    Folds in half while opening. Requires less space than swing gates.

  • Cantilever Gate

    Uses a cantilever arm to swing open without a ground track. Suitable for uneven surfaces.

Materials

Common wall gate materials include:

  • Wood (traditional, requires maintenance)
  • Metal (sturdy, with options like decorative wrought iron)
  • Vinyl (low-maintenance, affordable)
  • Composite (combines materials for durability and aesthetics with lower maintenance)

Additional Details

Consider these factors when choosing a wall gate:

  • Size: Accommodate vehicles or equipment passing through.
  • Style: Complement the overall property design.
  • Automation: Remote control or keypad for convenience (optional).
  • Security: Choose based on your property's security needs.
